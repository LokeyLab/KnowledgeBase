# Solution Phase Cyclization

## Overall Reaction

Solution-phase cyclization is a solution-phase amide bond formation reaction performed at low concentration to reduce dimerization. This reaction is most commonly performed in a solution of ACN/THF or one of the two solvents alone. Commonly used coupling reagents include COMU. Make sure that your peptide is free of potential capping agents which could prevent cyclization (Free amines and carboxylic acids in general, Ex: TFA, Piperidine). Be especially careful of potential capping agents when your cyclization is likely to be a slow reaction.

## Procedure

- Dilute peptide(s) to less than 3 mM concentration in 1:1 ACN:THF in a vessel containing a magnetic stir bar.
- Add 10 molar equivalents DIPEA, then make sure the peptide(s) are thoroughly dissolved.
- Add coupling reagent of choice (2eq COMU), let stir overnight.
- Evaporate to an oil.
- Purify if necessary.
