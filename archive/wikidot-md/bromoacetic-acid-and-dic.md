# Bromoacetic Acid and DIC Peptoid Coupling

## Overall Reaction

**NOTE:** Remember to deprotect your resin before starting!

## Step 1: Bromoacetic Addition

- Swell and drain resin.
- In a separate glass reaction vessel, make a 1M solution of bromoacetic acid in a volume of DMF that will cover your resin. Vortex until the bromoacetic acid is dissolved.
- Add DIC to the bromoacetic acid solution. The final concentration of DIC should be 0.5M giving a 2:1 solution of bromoacetic acid to DIC.
- Vortex and allow bromoacetic acid/DIC solution to rock for 20 minutes, and no more! Any longer and resin-cleaving side products can be formed. A urea precipitate should form, appearing as white crystals in a clear, colorless solution. Let the crystals settle for 5 minutes.
- Add the liquid of the bromoacetic acid/DIC solution to your resin. Use a pipette and try to avoid the crystals that have settled to the bottom of the reaction vessel. Do not dump all of the contents of the reaction vessel into your SPE tube.
- Cap SPE tube and shake for 45 minutes at room temperature.
- After 45 minutes, drain reaction mixture and wash resin with DMF (3x), 3:1 DCM:DMF (3x), and DCM (3x).
- Check reaction on LC/MS using Lokey Lab HPLC sample prep. Keep an eye out for the signature bromine spectrum caused by bromine's two naturally occurring isotopes (79 and 81 amu).

**NOTE:** Bromoacetic acid should be weighed out in the fume hood.

**NOTE:** Formation of the urea precipitate is not necessary for bromoacetic acid activation, but the precipitate is a helpful indication of bromoacetic acid activation. The precipitate is insoluble in DMF, but soluble in DCM. Any crystals accidentally added to resin will be removed in the DCM wash.

**PRO-TIP:** Don't allow this reaction to go on for more than one hour. The chance of unwanted/difficult to identify side reactions increases with time. Set your timers!

## Step 2: Amine Addition

- Swell resin in DCM.
- In a separate reaction vial, make a 1M solution of your amine.
- Drain DCM and immediately add amine solution to resin. Quickly cap and shake.
- Shake overnight. This can be done at room temperature or heated to 37C to push reaction to completion.
- Drain reaction mixture and wash resin with DMF (3x), 3:1 DCM:DMF (3x), and DCM (3x).
- Check reaction on LC/MS.

**PRO-TIP:** If the resin is not properly swollen or has a high loading value, dimers can form. Keep an eye out for this on your LC/MS runs.

**NOTE:** If you're in a hurry to make a peptide. The amine addition reaction time can be shortened to 3-4 hours. Reactions with most amines used so far go mostly to completion in this time frame.

Happy coupling!
