# Biotage Protocol

## Changing Solvents

1. Go to the Set up tab on the left-hand side of the screen.
2. From there you will be able to see two options: Solvents and Prime.
3. First go to the Solvents tab and switch the solvents to the ones you need.
4. Next take the solvents lines out of the currently used solvents and place them in the Acetone bottle.
5. From there, go to the Prime tab. Set the Path to without cartridge and both Solvent 1 and 2 are at 50%. 100mL is recommended for the priming the lines. Once these have been adjusted, hit Prime.
6. Once the acetone wash is complete, you can put the appropriate solvent lines in the designated solvents. Double check which solvents you designated as Solvent 1 and 2.
7. Repeat the Prime, this time with your desired solvents.

## Silica Gel Column, Dry Loading

1. Choose the size of your column, typically depends on the amount you are loading.
   a. 10g column- 50-100mg
   b. 25g column- 100-250mg
   c. 50g column- 250- 500mg
   d. 100g column- 500mg-1g

2. Once you've picked out the column size and find the appropriate parts (the column, two filters, the lid, and the weird thing that goes between the lid and filter). Make sure to do a good rinse with acetone.

3. Next add silica gel to the column, located next to the waste graveyard. Be sure to put a filter on the bottom of the column before putting the silica in, otherwise it will go through the bottom. When adding the silica be sure to slow and frequently tap the side of the tube to help the silica stack properly and get rid of any air. Note Make sure you didn't add to much silica because the lid won't be able to close properly and you'll have solvent leaking during purification.

4. Next change the solvent system to the appropriate solvents your purification needs.

5. Once your column is ready, see the New Method section to start your run.

## Reverse Phase, C18 Columns

For these column, you do not need to make your own. These are **reusable** and **expensive** column that we keep next to the Biotage. Please keep them capped on both ends when not hooked up to the Biotage. We use these to purify peptides as well as monomers and other compounds that use reverse phase purification, i.e. **Water/Acetonitrile.**

1. Choose the size of your column, typically chosen by the amount you need to purify:
   a. 12g -- 50mg to 100mg
   b. 30g -- 100mg to 400mg
   c. 100g -- 400mg or more

2. Next, change the solvents to Water and Acetonitrile, both with 0.1% TFA (Trifluoro Acetic Acid). Unless using the 100g column, use Methanol with 0.1% TFA instead of Acetonitrile.

3. Once ready to start your purification, see the New Methods section on how to set up your run.

## Starting a New Method

1. Once you have changed the Biotage to the appropriate solvents, begin creating your new method by going to the Method tab on the right-hand side of the menu. Once there hit New to start a new method. From there you will be prompted to choose the kind of column you are using. Remember this is a C18 ultra column for reverse phase.

2. From here you'll need to set the gradient conditions, the UV, the type of test tubes (150mm), and the name of the run. For the gradient, it is recommended to do three column volumes of the starting equilibrium.

3. Once you have adjusted the UV, gradient, and hooked up your column you can hit run.

4. Next load your sample.
   a. **For reverse phase**, you can load your sample by hitting load sample. If this step is forgotten, a back pressure will build and your product will spew out the top. Once you have pressed load sample, you can remove the top solvent line.
   b. **For regular phase**, it is recommended to inject directly on to the silica gel. To do this, get a piece of glass wear that you can collect excess solvent in. Place it below the column and then unscrew the bottom solvent line. This will allow the solvent to drip out of the column faster into the glass wear. Once the solvent has drained a little, remove the lid and the solvent spreader. Next, once the solvent level reaches the filter, take a glass pipet and add your product to the top of the filter. Let the solvent level reach right above the filter and reattach the bottom solvent line. Lastly reassemble the top of the column and attach the top solvent line. Be sure to not let your column run dry or your separation will be bad.

   When injecting, make sure that your compound is in a minimal amount of solvent. Excess amounts will give you poor separation.

5. Once your column is hooked back up to the Biotage, hit Gradient.

6. Once your column is running take the time to make sure that there aren't any leaks around the column because this could be an indicator of a part not being secured or one of the solvent lines being loose.
