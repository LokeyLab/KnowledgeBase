# BTC (Triphosgene Coupling)

## Procedure

- The amino acid to be coupled is weighed into a mail dry reaction vial/flask
- Triphosgene is weighed (under ventilation) and added to the vial
- While in the fume hood, dry solvent (THF or dichloromethane; do not use dimethylformamide) is added to the flask, sealed, agitated and allowed to react for 5-10 mins
- While in the fume hood, a non-nucleophilic base (2,6-lutidine or collidine) is added to quench any generated HCl, and agitated briefly. In volumes greater than 5mL, this step should be performed slowly in an ice bath.
- The resin is swelled with dry solvent (THF or dichloromethane), drained to a minimum of solvent volume, then charged with base (DIPEA or triethylamine)
- The reaction mixture is poured into the resin tube, sealed carefully, and agitated as long as necessary. Heating may be considered in a microwave, with special attention to prevent the solvent from evaporating to dryness.
- After completion, the resin is washed several times with dimethylformamide and dichloromethane to remove excess reagents

## Precaution

- Triphosgene is fatal if inhaled and causes severe skin burns and eye damage
- Triphosgene reacts with water to release phosgene gas. Make sure all reactions are handled in the fume hood.

## Reagent Information

**Triphosgene**
- Molecular weight: 296.75
- Density: 1.78 g/cm3
- Appearance: White crystal
- Melting Point: 80 Celsius
- Boiling Point: 206 Celsius
